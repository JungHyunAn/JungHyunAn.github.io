Research Interests

My work centers on robotic intelligence at the intersection of learning, control, and communication. I am broadly interested in developing principled and efficient methods that enable robots to generate structured behavior, adapt under constraints, and coordinate reliably in complex environments.

Current directions

  • Flow-matching and generative models for robot trajectory learning
  • Geometry-aware learning for constrained robotic data
  • Communication-aware control and coordination for multi-robot systems
  • Cloud, edge, and wireless support for embodied intelligence

Research

Generative Models for Robot Control

I study generative approaches to robot control, with a recent focus on flow-matching-based methods for structured and constrained robotic motion. My goal is to design learning methods that better exploit the geometry of robotic data for efficient and reliable trajectory generation.

Dexterous and Structured Manipulation

I am interested in robot learning methods for dexterous manipulation and structured action generation. In particular, I explore how task constraints and low-dimensional motion structure can be incorporated into generative policy learning.

Communication-Aware Multi-Robot Systems

I also work on communication-aware robotic systems, including wireless resource allocation, digital-twin-assisted simulation, and control-aware coordination for multi-robot settings. This research connects robotics with wireless communications and edge/cloud system design.

Long-Term Vision

My long-term goal is to build intelligent robotic systems that integrate learning, control, and communication in a unified way, enabling robots to act robustly both individually and as part of distributed systems.
